# 🎯 Overview

After understanding the importance of Data Analytics, Business Intelligence, and career opportunities in previous lectures, this lecture introduces the **real-world challenges faced during Data Analytics projects**.

In practical business environments, analysts rarely receive perfect, clean, and ready-to-use data.

Before creating reports, dashboards, or insights, analysts must overcome challenges related to:

* Data quality
* Data sources
* Business requirements
* Data accessibility
* Technology selection
* Performance
* Skills
* Change management
* Security
* Business execution

Understanding these challenges helps Data Analysts develop realistic expectations and prepare solutions before starting analytics projects.

# 📖 Lecture Summary

The instructor explains that successful Data Analytics projects require more than technical skills.

Even when organizations have powerful tools such as:

* Microsoft Excel
* SQL
* Power BI
* Business Intelligence platforms

projects can still fail because of poor data quality, unclear objectives, limited access, technical limitations, or lack of adoption.

1. Technical challenges
2. Business challenges

---

# 🔥 Why Understanding Challenges Matters

A common misconception among beginners is:

> "A Data Analyst only cleans data and creates dashboards."

In reality, professional analysts spend significant time:

* Understanding business problems.
* Preparing unreliable data.
* Connecting multiple systems.
* Communicating with stakeholders.
* Ensuring reports answer business questions.
* Helping organizations take action.

The ability to handle these challenges separates beginner analysts from professional analysts.

---

# 🚧 Common Challenges in Data Analytics Projects

---

# 1. Poor Data Quality

## 📌 Challenge

One of the biggest problems in analytics projects is receiving poor-quality data.

Data may be:

* Incomplete
* Incorrect
* Duplicate
* Outdated
* Inconsistent
* Missing important values

Poor-quality data produces unreliable analysis.

---

## 💼 Business Impact

If incorrect data is used:

* Reports become inaccurate.
* Decisions may be wrong.
* Business performance may be misunderstood.
* Resources may be wasted.

---

## 👨‍💻 Analyst Responsibility

Data Analysts must perform:

* Data cleaning
* Data validation
* Duplicate removal
* Missing value handling
* Data consistency checks

Example:

A sales report showing lower revenue may not mean sales decreased.

The problem could be:

* Missing transactions
* Incorrect pricing data
* Duplicate records

---

## 🛠 Tools Used

* Excel Cleaning Functions
* Power Query
* SQL Queries
* Power BI Data Transformation

---

# 2. Data Integration from Multiple Sources

## 📌 Challenge

Organizations store data across different systems.

Examples:

* Excel files
* Databases
* CRM systems
* ERP systems
* Cloud platforms

Combining this information can become complex.

---

## 💼 Business Example

A company wants a customer dashboard.

Customer information exists in:

| System         | Data             |
| -------------- | ---------------- |
| CRM            | Customer details |
| Sales System   | Purchases        |
| Finance System | Payments         |
| Website        | Online behavior  |

The analyst must combine all sources into one analytical model.

---

## 🛠 Possible Solutions

* ETL processes
* Data pipelines
* Power Query
* SQL joins
* Data modeling

---

# 3. Unclear Business Objectives

## 📌 Challenge

Sometimes organizations request reports without clearly defining:

* What problem needs solving?
* Which KPIs matter?
* What decisions should the report support?

---

## ❌ Example of Poor Requirement

"Create a sales dashboard."

This is unclear.

---

## ✅ Better Requirement

"Create a sales performance dashboard showing monthly revenue, top products, regional performance, and customer trends to help managers improve sales strategy."

---

## 💡 Analyst Responsibility

A Data Analyst must ask:

* What business question are we answering?
* Who will use this report?
* What decisions will it support?
* Which metrics matter?

---

# 4. Data Accessibility Problems

## 📌 Challenge

Analysts may not always have direct access to required data.

Common reasons:

* Security restrictions
* IT policies
* Permission issues
* Data ownership concerns

---

## Real-World Situation

An analyst wants automatic dashboard updates.

However:

* IT does not allow direct database connections.
* Data must be downloaded manually.
* Reports require repeated updates.

---

## Possible Solutions

* Proper access permissions
* Database connections
* Scheduled refresh
* Data governance processes

---

# 5. Complexity of Tools and Technologies

## 📌 Challenge

Choosing the correct technology is important.

Organizations must consider:

* Cost
* Scalability
* User skills
* Maintenance
* Future growth

---

## Example

A small business may not need an expensive enterprise BI system.

A solution using:

* Excel
* Power Query
* Power BI

may be sufficient.

---

# 6. Scalability and Performance Issues

## 📌 Challenge

As data volume increases, systems may become slow.

Problems include:

* Slow dashboards
* Long refresh times
* Poor user experience

---

## Example

A dashboard works perfectly with:

10,000 records

but becomes slow with:

10 million records.

---

## Solutions

Analysts improve performance through:

* Data optimization
* Better data models
* Removing unnecessary columns
* Optimized calculations
* Efficient queries

---

# 7. Lack of Skilled Resources

## 📌 Challenge

Organizations may have tools but lack skilled professionals.

Example:

A company purchases Power BI but has no employee who understands:

* Data modeling
* DAX
* Dashboard design
* Reporting practices

---

## Business Impact

Without skilled people:

* Tools remain unused.
* Reports are inaccurate.
* Analytics projects fail.

---

## Solution

Organizations invest in:

* Training
* Hiring analysts
* Building analytics teams

---

# 8. Resistance to Change

## 📌 Challenge

Employees may resist new technology or processes.

Common reactions:

* "The old method works."
* "We don't need a dashboard."
* "Excel reports are enough."

---

## Business Reality

Successful BI implementation requires:

* Training
* Communication
* User involvement
* Change management

---

# 9. Data Governance and Security

## 📌 Challenge

Organizations must protect sensitive information.

Examples:

* Customer data
* Financial information
* Employee records

---

## Important Areas

Data governance includes:

* Data ownership
* Access control
* Security policies
* Data quality standards

---

## Why It Matters

Without governance:

* Data can be misused.
* Privacy risks increase.
* Compliance problems occur.

---

# 10. Turning Insights into Action

## 📌 Challenge

Having reports does not automatically create business improvement.

Organizations must convert insights into actions.

---

## Example

Insight:

"Customer complaints increased by 20%."

Action:

* Investigate service problems.
* Improve support processes.
* Train employees.

---

## Important Concept

```
Data → Analysis → Insight → Decision → Action → Business Improvement
```

# 🎯 Interview Preparation

## Beginner Questions

### Q1. What are common challenges in Data Analytics projects?

**Answer:**

Common challenges include poor data quality, multiple data sources, unclear requirements, security issues, performance problems, lack of skills, and difficulty converting insights into business actions.

---

### Q2. Why is data quality important?

**Answer:**

Because inaccurate or incomplete data produces incorrect analysis and can lead to poor business decisions.

---

# Intermediate Questions

### Q3. How would you handle data coming from multiple sources?

**Answer:**

I would combine and transform data using ETL processes, Power Query, SQL joins, and proper data modeling techniques.

---

# Scenario-Based Question

## Scenario:

A manager says:

"We need a dashboard immediately."

But they cannot explain what decisions the dashboard should support.

What should you do?

### Answer:

First understand business requirements by discussing:

* Goals
* KPIs
* Target users
* Required decisions

Only after understanding the business need should the dashboard be designed.
